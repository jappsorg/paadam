## Session 1 findings (earlier):

What works well for a reluctant 6-year-old:
- Adventure mode intro screen (owl with stars, big colorful "Let's Go!" green button)
- "Animal Addition Adventure" content generated by Adventure mode: animal-themed, simple addition (3+2=5), approachable
- Friendly language in History: "1 so far -- nice!"
- The owl character "Ada" is appealing to kids
- Big colorful "Go! Make it!" button is clear and inviting

Critical pain points from session 1:
- GRADE/TOPIC SELECTION BUG: Button selections appear not to be reliably saved/applied. The worksheet generated was "Grade 5 Fractions" even though we attempted to pick 1st Grade Addition Easy. Selection state is extremely hard to read visually (tiny ring outline on selected vs unselected).
- PROBLEM COUNT BUG: Selected 5 problems, got 10 problems in the generated worksheet
- Selected button visual state is nearly invisible: only a thin outline ring differentiates selected from unselected. A 6-year-old cannot tell which options are chosen.
- The home screen category cards (Math Practice, Word Problems etc.) intercept click events from the worksheet form beneath them (layering/z-index bug causing mis-taps)
- Adventure mode "Getting ready..." state navigates to history tab without starting a new worksheet
- Insights tab is entirely parent-facing ("AI", "Accuracy", "Energy: Varies", "XP") -- no child-friendly framing at all
- "Insights" word itself is too advanced for Grade 1 reading level
- The worksheet form has too many options (13 topics!) for a 6-year-old to meaningfully choose from
- Word "Algebra", "Fractions", "Decimals" in topic list are 5th grade concepts being shown to 1st graders

## Session 2 findings (March 25, 2026) — Build My Own worksheet, Grade 1 Addition Easy, 5 questions:

### FIXES CONFIRMED since Session 1:
- Selection button visuals are MUCH better: selected grade/topic shows as pink filled pill, selected difficulty/count shows as yellow highlight. A 6-year-old can now clearly tell what is selected.
- Grade and topic correctly pre-fill to Jordan's profile (Grade 1, Addition already selected on load).
- Problem count now correctly generates 5 problems when 5 is selected (bug from session 1 is fixed).
- Topic list is filtered to grade-appropriate options for Grade 1 (Addition, Subtraction, Patterns, Measurement, Time, Money — no more Fractions/Algebra showing to 1st graders).
- "Change settings" collapsed view keeps form clean until needed.

### What works well (new findings):
- Ada the owl buddy changes her message on EVERY question ("Read carefully...", "Think about what you know...", "You can do this!", "Almost there, keep going!", "Outstanding job!"). Variety keeps it fresh.
- Ada's speech bubble turns GREEN after a correct answer — visual color change is delightful and noticeable.
- Ada gets a star icon next to her after a correct answer — subtle but satisfying.
- XP counter in top-right corner stays visible and ticks up (10, 20, 30, 40, 50 XP) through the worksheet — kids can watch it grow.
- "See My Results!" button on the last question is bigger/brighter than the regular Check button — the escalation signals it's special.
- Results page: large "100%" in pink, all-green Q1-Q5 cards, "+70 XP" and "1 day streak (+1!)" rewards clearly displayed.
- Home screen updates immediately after Done: streak fire shows 1, XP bar fills to 70/100, Addition bubble in "Your Journey" turns GREEN with gold star. Very satisfying loop.
- "30 more XP to level up!" message on profile creates a clear near-term goal.
- Profile shows Ada big and prominent — "Jordan's buddy" feels personal.
- Worksheet correctly generates 5 problems with Grade 1 Addition content (3+4, 6+2, word problems with simple numbers).
- "Question X of 5" counter is great — kids always want to know how many more.

## Session 3 findings (March 25, 2026, Round 2) — Build My Own worksheet, Grade 1 Addition Easy, 5 questions:

### FIXES CONFIRMED since Session 2:

- "See My Results!" button on question 5 is active and pressable by the child (not auto-clicked away).
- Ada remembers Jordan and gives a personalized nudge in the settings form ("Wow! You're really good at Addition! Ada thinks you're ready for Hard!").

### New things noticed (Round 2):
- XP counter does NOT appear until after the first question is answered — starts invisible, then shows "10 XP" badge after Q1. Slightly confusing on Q1.
- Per-question feedback (Correct! +10 XP) still auto-advances immediately — child never presses "Next Question" on Q1-Q4. Only Q5 ("See My Results!") is pressable. Partially improved.
- Ada says different message each question — good variety: "I believe in you!", "You can do this! One step at a time.", "Almost there, keep going!", "Wonderful! You're getting stronger!", "Excellent work! You nailed it!"

### Remaining pain points (Round 2):

**MEDIUM — Auto-advance still kills Next Question on Q1-Q4:**
- After answering Q1-Q4, the "Next Question" button appears DISABLED and app auto-advances. Child never gets to press it themselves. Only on Q5 does the button stay active. The satisfying "tap to continue" moment is missing for most questions.

**MEDIUM — No confetti / particle animation on achievement or 100% score:**
- The achievement modal has no confetti, sparkles, or animation. The 100% results screen has no fireworks. These are the peak emotional moments and they're visually static. A reluctant 6-year-old needs more sensory payoff here.

**MEDIUM — "Done" button blocked by overlay on Profile tab:**
- Navigating to Profile tab while on results screen causes the Done button to be intercepted by an invisible overlay element. Button appears but cannot be clicked.

**LOW — "0m Practice" counter never updates:**
- Home screen shows "0m Practice" even after completing a full worksheet. Timer appears broken or not wired up to actual session time.

**LOW — Buddy Bond bar barely moves after first worksheet:**
- After completing a full worksheet, "Buddy Bond" shows a tiny sliver of a red bar with "Just getting to know each other!" A kid who just finished work with Ada expects more warmth. The bond meter feels discouraging with so little progress shown.

**LOW — Streak stays 0 even after completing worksheet:**
- Day streak counter shows 0 on both home and profile even right after finishing. Likely a timezone/same-day logic issue but feels deflating.

**LOW — Results page hint tips are parent-facing:**
- Each question shows a light bulb tip like "Add the initial number of apples (2) to the apples received (5)". These read like teacher notes. A 6-year-old won't read or benefit from them. Could be replaced with a fun emoji or "Nice job!" sticker.

**Why:** These findings directly affect whether a reluctant 6-year-old feels rewarded enough to return. The achievement flow is now working and is the biggest improvement. The remaining gaps (auto-advance stealing button presses, no animation/confetti, broken practice timer) are the next priority.

**How to apply:** When reviewing engagement features, check: (1) does the child get to press buttons themselves at key moments? (2) do peak moments (100%, achievement unlock) have particle/animation payoff? (3) do all stats (practice time, streak) update correctly immediately after a session?

## Session 6 findings (March 28, 2026) — Auto-read-aloud feature code review (SpeechService, attempt screen, CharacterCompanion):

### What the auto-read feature DOES (code-confirmed):
- SpeechService wraps expo-speech; K and 1 grade get auto-read, grades 2-5 tap-only.
- Question is spoken 400ms after it appears on screen (useEffect on currentQuestionIndex).
- Speaker button (loudspeaker emoji) sits in a TouchableOpacity next to every question text — tapping toggles speak/stop.
- On correct answer: speaks "Correct! Great job!" aloud.
- On wrong answer: speaks "Not quite! The answer is [answer]." aloud.
- CharacterCompanion speaks its encouragement message for K-1 on every non-idle mood change.
- Character voice profiles: Ada = pitch 1.1 / rate 0.9 (calm), Max = pitch 1.2 / rate 1.05 (fast), Luna = pitch 1.15 / rate 0.85 (slowest).
- SpeechService.stop() is called on component unmount — no orphaned speech after leaving the screen.
- isSpeaking state is tracked so the speaker icon shows louder emoji while speaking.

### What WORKS WELL for a reluctant 6-year-old (code perspective):
- The 400ms auto-read delay is the right call — question renders first, THEN voice starts. No race condition.
- Speaking feedback aloud ("Correct! Great job!" / "Not quite! The answer is X") is excellent — a non-reader gets the result without having to decode text.
- CharacterCompanion speaks every non-idle message for K-1 — so Ada/Max/Luna "talks" throughout the session.
- Stop-on-unmount is a good safety net — no voice leaking into other screens.
- Speaker button is a TouchableOpacity right next to the question text — on mobile it will be reasonably tappable.

### CRITICAL GAPS that would still block a non-reading 6-year-old:

**CRITICAL — Home screen, navigation tabs, and mode cards are NOT spoken aloud.**
The auto-read only kicks in AFTER the child is inside the attempt screen. To GET there, the child must:
1. Read "Practice" / "Explore" / "Challenge" mode cards on the home screen — text only, no voice.
2. Read the tab bar labels ("History", "Profile") — text only.
3. If in Challenge mode: read and fill the configuration form ("How tricky?", "How many?") — text only.
4. If in Build My Own: select topic, difficulty, count — text only.
The attempt screen voice is an island. The PATH to the attempt screen is entirely text-dependent.

**CRITICAL — "Your Answer" text input label is NOT spoken.**
The TextInput has label="Your Answer". A Grade 1 non-reader who can't type will not know what to do with the input box. There's no voice prompt that says "type your answer here." The auto-read reads the QUESTION but not the affordance for answering.

**CRITICAL — Speaker button has no visual label — just a loudspeaker emoji.**
The button renders as a raw emoji (🔈 / 🔊) with no text. On mobile at 36px tap size it should be tappable, but it's positioned INSIDE the question row with no obvious affordance label. A 6-year-old might not associate a speaker emoji with "tap to hear it again." There's no tooltip, no pulsing animation, no "hear it again" label.

**HIGH — CharacterCompanion speaks companion messages AND question auto-read fires simultaneously.**
When a new question loads, two speech events can collide:
1. The companion's mood changes to "encouraging" → CharacterCompanion fires SpeechService.speak(companionMessage).
2. 400ms later, the question auto-read fires SpeechService.speak(questionText).
SpeechService.stop() is called before each new speak(), so the companion message gets CUT OFF by the question read. The kid hears "You can do th—" then immediately "Question 2: how many apples...". The companion's voice is wasted and feels glitchy.

**HIGH — Feedback explanation text ("💡 Add the initial apples...") is NOT spoken aloud.**
On wrong answers, the explanation is rendered as a Text element but only "Not quite! The answer is X" is spoken. The explanation (light bulb tip) is parent/teacher-facing text that a Grade 1 kid can't read. It adds visual clutter without benefit.

**HIGH — Results page is entirely text-dependent.**
When the worksheet finishes, the results screen shows:
- Score title ("Amazing!" / "Great job!" etc.)
- Score percentage
- XP and streak rewards
- Per-question review (Q1, Q2... with question text, user answer, correct answer)
- Action buttons ("My Past Scores", "Done")
None of this is spoken aloud. A non-reading Grade 1 kid just sees a wall of text. The character companion IS shown on results with "celebrating" mood — which DOES speak for K-1 — but only one message like "Outstanding job! I'm so proud!" The score itself, the reward text, the review cards — all silent.

**HIGH — "Previous" button navigation speaks nothing.**
If a kid presses Previous to go back to Q1, the question does NOT auto-re-read because the auto-read useEffect only fires on currentQuestionIndex change AND when autoRead && !showResults && !isLoading. Actually it DOES fire on index change. Wait — it does re-read. OK this one is fine.

**MEDIUM — isSpeaking state is internal to the attempt screen — NOT synced with expo-speech actual speaking state.**
SpeechService tracks a private `speaking` boolean that is set to false in onDone/onStopped/onError callbacks. But the attempt screen's `isSpeaking` state is set to true when speakQuestion() is called and reset in the onDone callback. If expo-speech fails silently (common on web), onDone may never fire — so isSpeaking stays true permanently, showing the louder speaker icon with no way to reset it except tapping again.

**MEDIUM — No voice on loading screen ("Loading your worksheet...").**
The LoadingState component shows text "Loading your worksheet..." — not spoken. A non-reader is left staring at a spinner with no audio cue that anything is happening.

**LOW — "Got it!" and "Next Question!" feedback buttons are text-only.**
After hearing "Correct! Great job!" aloud, the child still needs to READ "Next Question!" to know to press the green button. For a non-reader this is a stall point. An icon-only or icon+short word would help.

**LOW — Answer input IS disabled after checking (feedbackState !== null) — good.**
This is correct behavior. The input is locked while feedback is showing, so a kid can't accidentally overwrite.

### Net verdict for a reluctant non-reading 6-year-old:
The auto-read feature makes the INSIDE of the worksheet much more accessible. A kid who can't read CAN hear the question, hear correct/wrong feedback, and hear their buddy. BUT:
- They cannot get TO the worksheet without reading the home screen.
- They cannot understand the text input label.
- The companion speech gets cut off by the question read every time.
- The results page is entirely silent.
- The speaker button has no clear "tap me" affordance.

Jordan's honest rating of the auto-read feature: "kinda ok" (3/5). The voice reading the questions is GOOD. But I still can't find my way there without help. And Ada kept getting cut off right when she was about to say something nice.

**Why:** These are the true friction points for a K-1 non-reader using a voice-assisted app. The feature is real and functional but its scope is too narrow — it covers the question text but not the surrounding app shell.

**How to apply:** When evaluating voice/accessibility features, check: (1) does the voice cover the ENTIRE user journey, not just one screen? (2) do parallel speech events interfere with each other? (3) are interactive affordances (input boxes, buttons) also voiced or icon-labeled?

### KEY INSIGHT: The "selected learner" UI state and the "active learner for worksheet generation" are TWO DIFFERENT SYSTEMS that are not connected. Fixing the UI is not enough — the worksheet generation service must also read the selected learner's profile (grade, topics, difficulty) rather than defaulting to the first/logged-in user's child profile.
